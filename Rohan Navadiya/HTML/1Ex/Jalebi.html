Jalebi Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands (optional, for color and flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Batter:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, chickpea flour, baking powder, and turmeric powder.
    2. Add water gradually and whisk until you get a smooth, lump-free batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter. Cover and let it ferment for 8-10 hours or overnight.
  2. Prepare the Sugar Syrup:
    1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil and simmer until you get a one-string consistency syrup. Add saffron strands and cardamom powder for flavor (optional).
    2. Keep the sugar syrup warm on low heat while making the jalebis.
  3. Fry the Jalebi:
    1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan or kadhai over medium heat.
    2. Fill a piping bag fitted with a thin nozzle with the fermented batter.
    3. Hold the piping bag over the hot oil and squeeze to form concentric circles or swirls. Fry until they turn crispy and golden brown on both sides.
    4. Remove the jalebis using a slotted spoon and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for a few seconds and then transfer them to a serving plate.
  4. Serve:
    1. Serve the jalebis warm or at room temperature. They are best enjoyed fresh with a cup of hot milk or tea.
