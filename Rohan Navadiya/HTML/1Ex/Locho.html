Locho Recipe

Locho is a popular Gujarati snack made from gram flour (besan) and spices. It's a delicious and savory dish that's quite unique to the region. Follow the recipe below to make your own Locho at home!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1 cup yogurt (dahi)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon green chili paste (adjust according to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon fruit salt (eno)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Water as needed
  • Chopped coriander leaves for garnish
  • Grated coconut for garnish (optional)
  • Sev for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, semolina, yogurt, ginger paste, green chili paste, turmeric powder, cumin seeds, salt, and oil. Gradually add water to make a smooth batter.
  2. Steam the Batter: Grease a stainless steel plate or a thali with oil. Pour the batter into the greased plate/thali and steam for about 15-20 minutes until cooked through.
  3. Prepare the Locho: Once the batter is steamed, let it cool down slightly. Cut the steamed batter into small squares or desired shapes.
  4. Tempering (Optional): Heat oil in a small pan for tempering. Add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Pour this tempering over the prepared locho.
  5. Garnish: Garnish the locho with chopped coriander leaves, grated coconut, and sev (if using).
  6. Serve: Serve the delicious locho hot with green chutney or tamarind chutney.
