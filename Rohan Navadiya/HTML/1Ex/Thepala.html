Thepala Recipe

Thepala is a traditional Gujarati flatbread that is beloved for its delicious flavor and versatility. Made from whole wheat flour (atta) and flavored with fresh fenugreek leaves (methi) and spices, Thepala is a wholesome and nutritious dish enjoyed across Gujarat and beyond. Whether paired with yogurt, pickle, or enjoyed on its own, Thepala makes for a satisfying meal or snack at any time of the day. Follow this simple recipe to create your own batch of soft and flavorful Thepalas right in your kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/4 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup fresh fenugreek leaves (methi), finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup yogurt (plain)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (optional, adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil or ghee, for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, gram flour, chopped fenugreek leaves, ginger paste, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin seeds, carom seeds, salt, and 1 tablespoon of oil.
    2. Mix the ingredients well using your hands until they resemble coarse crumbs.
    3. Gradually add yogurt and knead the mixture into a smooth dough. If the dough is too dry, add a little water at a time until you get a soft, pliable dough.
    4. Cover the dough with a clean kitchen towel and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.
  2. Divide and Roll:
    1. After resting, divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
    2. Take one dough ball and roll it out into a thin, round disc using a rolling pin. Dust the rolling surface and the dough with flour as needed to prevent sticking.
  3. Cook Thepala:
    1. Heat a tawa or skillet over medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled out Thepala onto the tawa.
    2. Cook for about 1-2 minutes on one side until you see bubbles forming on the surface.
    3. Flip the Thepala and drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and on the surface.
    4. Press gently with a spatula and cook until both sides are golden brown and cooked through.
    5. Repeat the same process with the remaining dough balls until all Thepalas are cooked.
  4. Serve:
    1. Serve Thepalas hot with yogurt, pickle, or any chutney of your choice.
    2. They can also be packed for lunch or enjoyed as a snack.
  5. Storage:
    1. Thepalas can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. You can also freeze them for longer storage.
